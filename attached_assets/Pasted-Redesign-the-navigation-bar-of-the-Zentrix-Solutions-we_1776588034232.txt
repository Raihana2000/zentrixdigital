Redesign the navigation bar of the Zentrix Solutions website to look more premium, clean, and high-end, similar to modern SaaS companies (Stripe, Vanta).

GOAL:
The navbar must feel calm, balanced, and professional. The logo should stand out as the main brand element, while the rest of the navigation supports it — not competes with it.

---

1. LAYOUT & HIERARCHY

- Left side:
  Logo + brand name "Zentrix" (main visual focus)

- Center:
  Navigation links:
  Home / Over ons / Diensten / Contact

- Right side:
  Language toggle (NL / EN)
  CTA button (subtle, not overpowering)

Make sure the layout is symmetrical and balanced.

---

2. LOGO PRIORITY

- Keep the logo larger than other elements
- Ensure it is the strongest visual anchor
- Add enough spacing around it so it feels premium

---

3. NAVIGATION LINKS (IMPORTANT)

- Reduce visual weight of navigation links
- Use softer white (not pure white, slightly muted like text-gray-300)
- Font size slightly smaller than brand text
- Add smooth hover effect:
  → text becomes brighter or slightly blue

- Add subtle underline or glow on hover (very minimal)

---

4. CTA BUTTON (FIX THIS — VERY IMPORTANT)

Current issue: CTA is too dominant

Update it:

- Reduce size slightly (padding smaller)
- Use softer blue (not too bright)
- Add subtle border or glow instead of heavy fill
- Make it feel premium, not aggressive

Alternative:
- Use outline button style:
  border-blue-500 + hover fill

Text:
"Neem contact op"

---

5. LANGUAGE TOGGLE (NL / EN)

- Make it minimal and clean
- Use small text or pill style
- Highlight active language subtly (blue or underline)
- Do not let it visually compete with CTA

---

6. NAVBAR BACKGROUND

- Use dark background (#0B0B0B or slightly transparent)
- Add subtle blur effect (backdrop-blur)
- Add thin bottom border (border-white/10)
- Optional: slight shadow for depth

---

7. SPACING & PADDING

- Increase horizontal padding (px-6 to px-10 desktop)
- Increase vertical padding slightly (py-3 to py-4)
- Ensure equal spacing between elements

---

8. RESPONSIVENESS (VERY IMPORTANT)

DESKTOP:
- Clean horizontal layout
- Good spacing between logo, links, CTA

TABLET (iPad):
- Reduce spacing slightly but keep balance
- Ensure logo stays prominent

MOBILE:
- Show logo clearly on left
- Hamburger menu on right
- Keep CTA minimal or inside menu if needed
- Do NOT shrink logo too much

---

9. INTERACTIONS

- Smooth transitions (duration-200 / ease-in-out)
- Hover effects must be subtle and premium
- No flashy animations

---

10. FINAL FEEL

The navbar should feel:
- Calm
- Premium
- Controlled
- High-end tech company

NOT:
- crowded
- aggressive
- cheap
- oversized

---

IMPORTANT:
The logo is the hero of the navbar.
Everything else must support it, not compete with it.
